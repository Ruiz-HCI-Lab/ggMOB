ggMOB-Net: Elucidation of Genomic Conjugative Features and Associated Cargo Genes across Bacterial Genera Using Genus-Genus Mobilization Networks

Gowri Nayar, Ignacio Terrizzano, Ed Seabolt, Akshay Agarwal, Christina Boucher, Jaime Ruiz, Ilya B Sizovskiy, James H Kaufman, and Noelle Noyes

${require('./includes/_abstract.html')}

View Visualization

Highlights

  • We construct a frequency-based mobile network showing genus-genus conjugation features and cargo genes from >200,000 publicly available bacterial genomes, representing 1,345 different genera.
  • We make the resulting computational method which allows the network to be queried and visualized publicly available under the GNU v3 license: . We refer to our method as (ggMOB-Net).
  • We show how ggMOB-Net can be used to provide insight into conjugative mobilization between unculturable bacteria spanning multiple genera.
  • In particular, we observe that the ratio of AMR gene representation in conjugative versus non-conjugative genomes exceeded 5:1, confirming that conjugation is a critical force for antimicrobial resistance spread across genera.
